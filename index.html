About BTS

BTS, also known as Bangtan Sonyeondan or Beyond The Scene, is a South Korean boy band that has taken the global music scene by storm since their debut in 2013. Comprising seven talented members—RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook—BTS is renowned for their innovative music that blends various genres, including pop, hip-hop, and R&B. Their lyrics often tackle themes of youth, mental health, and self-empowerment, resonating deeply with fans worldwide. With a massive and dedicated fanbase known as ARMY, BTS has broken numerous records, including being the first K-pop group to top the Billboard Hot 100 chart. Their influence extends beyond music, as they actively advocate for social issues, making them not just artists but also cultural icons.

Achievements

BTS has achieved remarkable success both in South Korea and internationally, solidifying their status as one of the most influential music groups in the world. They are the first K-pop act to top the Billboard Hot 100 chart, with multiple songs reaching this milestone, including "Dynamite," "Butter," and "Permission to Dance." The group has won numerous awards, including Billboard Music Awards, American Music Awards, and MTV Video Music Awards, showcasing their global appeal and recognition. BTS has also set several Guinness World Records, including the record for the most Twitter engagements for a musical group. Their impact extends beyond music; they have been recognized for their philanthropic efforts, including their partnership with UNICEF for the "Love Myself" campaign, which aims to end violence against children and teenagers. Through their music and activism, BTS continues to inspire millions around the globe, making significant contributions to both the entertainment industry and social causes.
